# Week 10 — Challenges

One challenge this week, and it is the whole point of the course: take the full stack from a clean clone to a live, sign-in-and-use demo through one documented pipeline run. No solution is provided — only acceptance criteria. The challenge subsumes the mini-project's build and adds the *cold-start* bar: a grader (or a teammate, or future-you) deploys the entire system reading only your runbook, with one pipeline run, and signs in on a phone.

| # | File | What you prove | Est. time |
|---|------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | [challenge-01-clone-to-live-demo.md](./challenge-01-clone-to-live-demo.md) | A clean `git clone` becomes a live, signed-in, working Crunch Tracker through one documented GitHub Actions run, plus the runbook another engineer could deploy from. | The capstone itself (multi-day) |

This is the headline portfolio artifact. Treat it like a release, not an assignment. The single hardest gate is the *cold start*: if the deploy only works because of steps that live in your head, it does not pass. Write the runbook as you build, and have someone who has never seen your project try to follow it.
